# Travel and Expense Policy

Employees should use the company booking tool whenever practical for air, hotel, and rail reservations. Booking outside the approved channel requires a documented business reason.

Domestic travel meals are reimbursable up to $45 per travel day. International travel meals are reimbursable up to $65 per travel day unless a lower local cap is published by Finance.

Hotel bookings should stay within the city rate guidance published by Finance. If no preferred rate is available, employees may book the lowest reasonable room in a safe location near the business destination.

Receipts are required for all expenses of $25 or more and for every lodging expense regardless of amount. Expense reports must be submitted within 10 calendar days of the end of travel.

Alcohol is not reimbursable unless it is part of an approved customer event and the expense is pre-approved by a vice president or above.

Personal travel combined with business travel must be clearly separated in the booking records and expense submission. The company reimburses only the business-related portion.
