# Security and Access Policy

Every employee must use multifactor authentication for company email, code repositories, HR systems, customer support systems, and any service that stores confidential information.

Phishing or suspicious email messages must be reported to the Security team within 30 minutes of discovery using the dedicated incident reporting channel. Employees must not click links, open attachments, or forward suspicious messages to other employees except Security.

Production access is granted on the principle of least privilege. Managers may request access for business reasons, but Security must approve access to production systems containing customer data or security logs.

Software vendors that process customer data must complete security review and contract review before purchase or implementation. Security and Legal must both approve tools that store, transmit, or analyze customer data.

Shared credentials are prohibited. Service accounts must be documented, rotated, and assigned to a responsible owner.

Lost or stolen devices that may have accessed company systems must be reported immediately and no later than one hour after discovery.
