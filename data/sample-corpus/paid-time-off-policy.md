# Paid Time Off Policy

Full-time employees accrue paid time off each pay period according to their employment level and local benefits schedule. PTO may be used for vacation, personal time, and short personal appointments.

Employees should submit PTO requests through the HR system as early as possible. Requests for one to four days of PTO should be submitted at least three business days in advance. Requests for five or more consecutive business days must be submitted at least 15 business days in advance unless there is an emergency.

Managers approve PTO based on business coverage needs, fairness across the team, and any blackout periods that have been announced in advance. PTO is not guaranteed until manager approval is recorded in the HR system.

Unused PTO carries over into the next calendar year only up to the local legal maximum or the company carryover cap, whichever is lower. Any balance above that amount is forfeited unless local law requires an exception.

Employees who are sick for more than three consecutive workdays may be asked to provide documentation in accordance with local law and HR guidance.

Company-recognized holidays are separate from PTO and should not be entered as vacation days.
