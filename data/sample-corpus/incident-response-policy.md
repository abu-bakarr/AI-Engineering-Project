# Incident Response Policy

Security incidents are classified into severity levels from SEV-1 through SEV-4 based on customer impact, legal exposure, and operational disruption.

A SEV-1 incident requires immediate paging of the incident commander, Security, Engineering leadership, and Customer Support leadership. The first incident log entry must be created within 15 minutes of declaring the incident.

The incident commander is responsible for maintaining the timeline, assigning owners, and confirming customer communication checkpoints. Engineers should avoid making unrelated changes during an active incident.

Customer-facing updates for SEV-1 and SEV-2 incidents must be issued at least every 60 minutes until the incident is resolved or a different communication cadence is approved by the incident commander.

A written post-incident review is required for every SEV-1 and SEV-2 incident within five business days of resolution. The review must include root cause, impact, corrective actions, and accountable owners.

Evidence related to security incidents must be preserved according to Legal and Security guidance and may not be deleted or altered outside the approved response process.
